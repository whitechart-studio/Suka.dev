# FAQ

## Is Suka a code editor?

No. Suka is a coordination layer for teams and agents working around a codebase.

## Is Suka local or hosted?

Today, Suka is local-first. Hosted and self-hosted team modes are planned.

## Does Suka upload source code?

No by default. Suka is designed around coordination metadata such as paths, claims, events, and decisions.

## Which agents does Suka support?

The architecture is agent-agnostic. The near-term goal is to support Codex, Cursor, terminal agents, and future adapters through CLI/API publishing.

## Why use claims?

Claims make work ownership visible before conflicts happen. They help agents avoid stepping on the same paths, APIs, or domains.

## Why a repo map?

Agentic development needs spatial awareness. A repo map makes active work, risk, and decisions visible in one operational surface.
